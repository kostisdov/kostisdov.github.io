---
title: "Why write anything down at all"
description: "A short apology for keeping a public notebook in an age of infinite content."
date: 2026-06-08
tags: ["meta"]
---

I have spent most of my working life producing documents nobody reads for pleasure:
specifications, design reviews, papers with seventeen pages of appendices. This site is an
experiment in the opposite direction.

The premise is simple. I understand things by writing them down, and most of what I write
down ends up in notebooks that even I never reopen. Publishing is a forcing function — a
half-understood idea survives a private notebook just fine, but it does not survive an
audience, even a hypothetical one.

So the plan is to keep a public notebook. Some entries will be technical, with equations and
all — the mathematics behind radios is genuinely beautiful and almost nobody gets to see it.
Some will not be technical at all. I make no promises about cadence, theme, or coherence.

> A note is a thing you write for yourself; a post is a note you were forced to finish.

That's the whole manifesto. Let's see how it goes.
